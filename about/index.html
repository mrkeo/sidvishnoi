About

I'm an independent software engineer and full-stack web developer, with a focus on front-end development. I love to work with JavaScript and C++, and have a growing interest in Rust. I'm more commonly known by my pseudonym, Sid Vishnoi.

I bring extensive experience in front-end technologies, including in-depth knowledge of ECMAScript, CSS, and HTML. I also have experience working with common frameworks, such as React. I’m adept at learning new languages and technologies. I’m also very comfortable with Git, GitHub, and open-source workflows, code review, test-driven development, and the tooling in general.

I've been coding since 2009, and decided to take on software development professionally in 2016. In 2018, I was a Google Summer of Code scholar for Mozilla. Since then, I’ve continued to be an active contributor in the open-source community, where I’ve focused on bringing new tools and services that are widely in use at the W3C. I presented my W3C tooling-related work at W3C TPAC 2019 in Fukuoka, Japan. In 2020, I was awarded Mozilla Open Source Support (MOSS) award to work on an open-source project.

While not at computer, I take a more human form. I like to watch sunset, clouds and birds, and take care of my terrace garden.
